Meet Emio Tomeoni, Stephanie Ramos Husband

June 2024 · 3 minute read

In the wake of the original cohosts’ dating scandal, Gio Benitez and Stephanie Ramos were chosen to temporarily replace Amy Robach and T.J. Holmes on Good Morning America. Emio Tomeoni, Stephanie’s husband, will be introduced as you scroll down to find out more about their relationship and family.

Who is Emio Tomeoni, Stephanie Ramos’ husband?

Emio is a producer, editor, director, and cinematographer. Emio Created Media, a firm that produces videos, was founded by him and debuted in February 2014. Emio described the business via his LinkedIn page: “With an exclusive clientele, we work only for enterprises that depend as much on the personality of the business owner as the business itself.” “We work for you if your business is entwined with the stories of your family and life, if your name is the company name, and if a client has ever written you a thank-you note personally.”

How long have Emio Tomeoni and Stephanie Ramos been married?

Emio and Stephanie exchanged vows on September 4, 2010. Xavier and Gio are their joint sons.

The majority of Stephanie’s social media posts feature her career as a writer, however in September 2021, she posted on Instagram to commemorate their 11th wedding anniversary.

What Position Did Stephanie Ramos Hold at ABC Prior to Taking Over “GMA3”?

On December 5, 2022, Stephanie will temporarily take over hosting duties on GMA3. She was a national correspondent for ABC News before to that. She has reported on stories including the military and mental health crises in Latinx communities during her time with the network.

The New Yorker previously held positions as an assignment editor in South Carolina and a news anchor in Missouri before joining ABC in 2015.

How long will Gio Benitez and Stephanie Ramos co-host the Grammys?

The length of Stephanie and Gio’s tenure as GMA3 cohosts is presently unknown.

They were chosen to step in when Amy and T.J. were benched due to relationship rumors, according to In Touch.

The cohosts, who have been dating since 2020, were first romantically linked on November 30 just hours later, according to many insiders who spoke with In Touch. The circumstance, which has been referred to as “an HR nightmare,” is one that ABC is “desperately attempting to find out how to address.”

The network is being “cautious” about the new affair, which the author of Better and a former CNN personality hope to make “official,” according to a different source who previously spoke to In Touch.

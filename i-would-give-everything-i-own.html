Overall Meaning

The song "Everything I Own," originally recorded by the band Bread in 1972, is a poignant tribute to a lost loved one. The song is written from the perspective of someone who has lost someone they love dearly, and would do anything to have them back in their life. The opening lines speak to the person's gratitude for all the ways in which their loved one sheltered and protected them throughout their life. They acknowledge that the best years of their life were the ones they spent with this person, and that they owe everything to them.


The chorus of the song is where the true message behind the lyrics comes through. The person singing the song is saying that they would give up anything they own to have their loved one back in their life. Their life, their heart, their home - all of these things are meaningless without the person they love. The second verse goes on to talk about how the person they're singing to taught them how to love, just by being an example, rather than by saying too much. The singer of the song is still holding on to the memories of their time with this person, and no one else can understand the depth of their loss.


The final verse of the song is a warning to anyone who might be taking someone they love for granted. The singer implores the listener to appreciate their loved ones while they still have them, because they could be taken away at any moment. The song ends with the same sentiment as the chorus - the singer would give anything they own just to have their loved one back in their life.
